The Future of Dollar Dominance

An Analysis of Emerging Threats to Dollar Hegemony

Fritz Albers, Monica Gronseth, Lizzie Heywood, Colin Hughes, Duyen Pham  ·  April 2026

Watch: Video Report

The dollar is the world's premier currency, but it is under threat. We have identified three forces that could affect its dominance over the coming decade and beyond, including the rise of digital currencies, the growing international role of China's yuan, and declining global trust US economic policies and institutions. To be sure, the dollar is still dominant and remains the world's most important currency. However, these forces will challenge its preeminence in the years to come, and the US should begin considering policy adjustments that prevent its decline.

Key Findings

  • 01
    Digital currencies could act as a digital disruptor. The rapid expansion of digital currencies (e.g., $33 trillion stablecoin transaction volume in 2025) poses a challenge to dollar hegemony.
  • 02
    The yuan is gaining ground. The Chinese yuan is gaining traction in global trade and finance, accounting for over 50% of China's cross-border payments and approximately 8% of global trade finance. Its steady adoption signals a shift towards a more multipolar currency system.
  • 03
    Trust in the US dollar is diminishing. Domestic and foreign policy (e.g., whipsawing trade policy, eroding rule of law, attacks on the Fed), along with ballooning national debt, is causing weakened global trust in the dollar.
  • 04
    The US should act now. It should consider regulating and integrating dollar-backed stablecoins, modernizing cross-border payment infrastructure, and restoring fiscal credibility through institutional stability and long-term debt management.
