The Future of Dollar Dominance

An Analysis of Emerging Threats to Dollar Hegemony

The dollar is the world's premier currency, but it is under threat. We have identified three forces that could affect its dominance over the coming decade and beyond, including the rise of digital currencies, the growing international role of China's yuan, and declining global trust US economic policies and institutions. To be sure, the dollar is still dominant and remains the world's most important currency. However, these forces will challenge its preeminence in the years to come, and the US should begin considering policy adjustments that prevent its decline.

Key Findings

  • 01
    Digital currencies are building alternative rails. Stablecoin transaction volumes hit $33 trillion in 2025, and CBDCs like China's digital yuan are designed to bypass SWIFT — weakening the dollar's network effects and offering an off-ramp for countries facing U.S. sanctions.
  • 02
    The yuan is gaining ground. The Chinese yuan now accounts for over 50% of China's cross-border payments and roughly 8% of global trade finance, signaling a gradual shift toward a more multipolar currency system.
  • 03
    Trust in U.S. institutions is eroding. Policy volatility, attacks on the Fed's independence, and a debt trajectory projected to reach 156% of GDP — capped by a Moody's downgrade — are chipping away at the confidence that underpins dollar dominance.
  • 04
    The U.S. should act now. To recommend regulating and integrating dollar-backed stablecoins, modernizing cross-border payment infrastructure, and restoring fiscal credibility through institutional stability and long-term debt management.
